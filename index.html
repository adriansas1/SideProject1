The Lockheed Martin F-22 Raptor is one of the most advanced stealth jet fighter of 5th gen (fiflth generation) developed for the United States Air Force (USAF).

An F-22 Raptor flies over Kadena Air Base, Japan, on a routine training mission in 2009.


Overview

The F-22 has a range of special features: thrust-vectoring, twin-engine, cockpit, all-weather stealth tactical.


Thrust vectoring

Thrust vectoring is built into the flight control system, so it works automatically in response to commands from the pilot.



Twin engine - Pratt & Whitney F119 engine

The F119-PW-100 turbofan is the first operational fifth generation fighter engine. It combines stealth technologies and vectored thrust with high thrust-to-weight performance to provide unprecedented maneuverability and survivability.

The F119 is equipped with a number of advanced technologies for unmatched operational performance and reliability. Cockpit


The F-22's cockpit is one of the very first "all-glass" cockpits for tactical fighters - there are no traditional round dial, standby or dedicated gauges. It accommodates the largest range of pilots (the central 99 percent of the Air Force pilot population) of any tactical aircraft.
